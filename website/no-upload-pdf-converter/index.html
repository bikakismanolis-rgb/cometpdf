FAQ

Does CometPDF upload my files?

No. CometPDF is designed as a local Windows desktop app. The files you select are converted on your computer, not sent to a CometPDF server.

Is it safer than an online PDF converter?

For private documents, local conversion reduces the risk of exposing files to a third-party upload service. You still need to use normal computer security practices, but the conversion itself stays local.

Does no upload mean it works offline?

Yes. After installation, normal conversion does not require an internet connection. You can convert supported files even when the computer is offline.

Can it convert PDFs back to other formats?

Yes. CometPDF 1.1 can export PDFs to PNG, JPG, TXT, DOCX, and PPTX. Some outputs are visual exports, so OCR-based fully editable reconstruction is planned for a future Pro version.

Why does Windows show a warning?

CometPDF is not code-signed yet, so Windows SmartScreen or Chrome may show an unknown publisher warning. Check the Security page for current SHA256 hashes and VirusTotal scan notes, or use the Portable ZIP without installation.
