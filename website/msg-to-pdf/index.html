Convert Outlook MSG to PDF offline.

CometPDF converts Outlook MSG and EML email files to PDF on Windows. It first tries a print-like route through Outlook and Word, then falls back to a clean email PDF with subject, sender, recipients, date, and body text.

This is not an online email converter. CometPDF converts MSG and EML files locally with a Windows desktop app.

How it works

  1. Install CometPDF for Windows.
  2. Add an Outlook MSG file, an EML file, or use right-click Convert to PDF.
  3. The email PDF is created locally on the computer.

The output is designed for practical email archiving. When Outlook and Word can render the message, CometPDF uses that local path for a print-like result; otherwise it creates a structured PDF that captures the most important email fields.

Useful for email archiving

Emails often need to be saved as PDFs for legal records, project documentation, client communication archives, accounting evidence, order histories, or compliance workflows. Uploading MSG files to an online converter is a privacy risk because email files can contain personal data, attachments, addresses, signatures, internal notes, and confidential correspondence.

CometPDF converts MSG and EML files locally on Windows. When Microsoft Outlook and Word are available, it uses their rendering path for a print-like result. If not, it falls back to a clean structured PDF with subject, sender, recipients, date, and body text. This gives users a simple way to archive email content without sending mail files to a third-party server.

Also supports EML

CometPDF can also convert EML email files into readable PDF documents. EML support is useful for users who export messages from non-Outlook email clients or need a common PDF copy of individual email records.

FAQ

Do I need Outlook installed?

Outlook gives the best rendering for MSG files. CometPDF uses Outlook and Word together for a print-like email PDF when they are available, and falls back to a clean structured email PDF when they are not.

What is the difference between MSG and EML?

MSG is the native format for Microsoft Outlook emails. EML is used by many other email clients, including Thunderbird, Apple Mail exports, and standard email archive workflows.

Does it upload email files?

No. MSG and EML files are converted entirely on your Windows computer. Nothing is sent to a server during conversion.

Can I convert many emails at once?

Yes. Batch conversion is supported. Add multiple MSG or EML files and convert them in one run.

Can email attachments be included in the PDF?

CometPDF converts the email body and key message fields to PDF. Attachments are separate files and are not embedded into the PDF output in this release.
